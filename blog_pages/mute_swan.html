History and Natural Significance

The UEA lake is absolutely perfect swan habitat, and our resident pair clearly knows it. Mute Swans love shallow, nutrient-rich water with plenty of aquatic vegetation, and our lake ticks all those boxes. The male I was watching was probably defending his territory - during breeding season, male swans become incredibly aggressive about protecting their patch of water and their mate.

Also can we just talk about how misleading the name "Mute Swan" is? Because the individual I was watching was making more noise than most of the students walking past! They're called "mute" not because they're silent, but because they don't have the trumpeting calls that other swan species make. Instead, they communicate through hisses, grunts, snorts, and these weird vibrating sounds.
