Earlham Park, Spring 2025

History and Natural Significance

UEA's older buildings and sheltered outdoor areas are absolutely perfect for these spiders. They love those quiet corners where they won't be disturbed - I've found them under bench overhangs, in the gaps between building extensions, and tucked away in the more sheltered parts of the campus where there's less foot traffic.

When I first heard we had Noble False Widow spiders (Steatoda nobilis) living around UEA campus, I was a bit freaked out. The media has really done a number on these poor creatures with all the "Britain's most venomous spider" headlines.

These spiders are actually quite important predators in their adopted ecosystem. They're catching loads of flies, mosquitoes, and other insects that would otherwise be bothering us. They've also become part of the food web here - I've seen birds taking them occasionally, and they probably provide food for other predators too. It's a great example of how introduced species can sometimes integrate quite well into existing ecosystems.
