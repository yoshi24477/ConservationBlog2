History and Natural Significance

Right, so can we talk about how confusing this plant's name is? It's called "Green" Alkanet but has brilliant blue flowers - what's that about? Turns out the "green" bit refers to the leaves staying green all year round (that's what sempervirens means in Latin - "always green"). The flowers are this stunning electric blue that practically glows in the dappled shade where it loves to grow.

Here's the thing about Green Alkanet - it's not actually native to Britain. It's originally from the western Mediterranean, but it's been here so long (probably since medieval times) that it's completely naturalised. It was originally grown in monastery gardens and cottage gardens, probably for medicinal uses, but like so many garden plants, it decided life was better on the outside.

Green Alkanet belongs to the Boraginaceae family (the borage family), and once you know this, you can spot the family resemblances everywhere.
