Decomposers of little earth

History and Natural Significance

Who would have thought that some of the most fascinating creatures on campus would be the ones most people completely ignore or accidentally step on? I'm talking about pill woodlice (Armadillidium vulgare) - those little grey "roly-polies" that roll up into perfect balls when you disturb them.

Here's something that blew my mind - these aren't insects at all! They're crustaceans, more closely related to crabs and shrimp than to beetles or ants. When you look at them closely, you can actually see the resemblance. They've got those segmented bodies, multiple pairs of legs, and even gills! Actual gills! They're basically tiny land lobsters wandering around campus.

These little guys are fantastic when it comes to recycling. They're basically nature's composting team, munching their way through dead leaves, rotting wood, and other organic matter that would otherwise just pile up everywhere.
