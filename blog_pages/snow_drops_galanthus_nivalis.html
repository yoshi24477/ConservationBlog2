History and Natural Significance

What amazes me most about snowdrops is their sheer determination. While everything else is still hiding underground waiting for warmer weather, these incredible little bulbs are already pushing through, sometimes even through snow (hence the name!). I found clusters of them around campus in various stages.

UEA's campus is brilliant habitat for snowdrops. They love the kind of semi-shaded areas we have around the older buildings and along the woodland edges. I've found them scattered throughout campus, but the best displays are definitely in areas where they've been left undisturbed for a few years to really establish themselves.

Snowdrops aren't actually native to Britain - they were probably introduced centuries ago, possibly by monks who valued them for their early flowering and symbolic association with hope and renewal. But they've become so much a part of our landscape that it's hard to imagine British winters without them.
